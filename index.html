This isn't a campaign you rent. It's an enrollment engine FIU owns — five connected services that grow your ten priority programs and produce the evidence the Board is asking for, in the same motion.

The Problem

For FIU's self-supporting programs, enrollment is the foundation everything else rests on. On September 18, the Board heard the portfolio's overall enrollment tick up 2% — but underneath that average, these ten priority programs are losing students. The mandate is full-cost recovery; with expenditures still exceeding revenues, a $20.3M reserve is the only thing buffering the gap, and the Board is now asking which programs can sustain themselves.

But the real stakes sit beyond the balance sheet: these are the programs that build and renew South Florida's workforce. A program cut for under-enrollment isn't a budget line removed — it's a regional talent pipeline lost.

Two of the three levers are already shut — no market-rate tuition increase in over a decade, and these programs were left out of the Board of Governors' out-of-state increase. Volume is the lever still open. Yet today there's no system to grow these programs deliberately, no evidence base to defend them, and the one thing that should make them impossible to overlook — Miami — is absent from how they go to market.

The Solution

First stabilize, then grow — deliberately — so these programs can keep supplying the regional talent pipeline and become sustainable. Build an engine, not a campaign — one system, built once and run continuously, that grows enrollment and produces the proof in the same motion. FIU owns all of it.

We prove the engine on your first ten programs. Once it's built, the same system extends across FIU's ~70 programs at a fraction of the cost — the narrative, infrastructure, and tracking already paid for. The pilot stands on its own; everything after it is economies of scale.

How the cut-or-grow call actually works
Incremental in, incremental out.

FIU already paid for the hard part — faculty, curriculum, accreditation, platform. Those costs are sunk and identical with or without us, so they leave the decision. Our fee is the only new cost; the incremental student is the return.

The fixed cost is the moat, not the liability.

Because those costs are already covered, every empty seat is stranded margin. The marginal student is the single most profitable enrollment FIU can book — which is why contribution per student clears our cost so easily.

The loop is what makes it an engine: intelligence feeds back into the story, so every cycle gets sharper and more efficient — and it never turns off, because you own it.
