The Problem

FIU's auxiliary programs sustain themselves on enrollment — and the Board is asking which ones can. But the real stakes sit beyond the balance sheet: these are the programs that build and renew South Florida's workforce.

A program cut for under-enrollment isn't a budget line removed — it's a regional talent pipeline lost. Yet today there's no system to grow these programs deliberately, no evidence base to defend them, and the one thing that should make them impossible to overlook — Miami — is absent from how they go to market.

The Solution

Build an engine, not a campaign — one system, built once and run continuously, that grows enrollment and produces the proof in the same motion. FIU owns all of it.

We prove the engine on your first ten programs. Once it's built, the same system extends across FIU's ~70 programs at a fraction of the cost — the narrative, infrastructure, and tracking already paid for. The pilot stands on its own; everything after it is economies of scale.

The loop is what makes it an engine: intelligence feeds back into the story, so every cycle gets sharper and cheaper — and it never turns off, because you own it.
